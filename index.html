The size of the footprint of a country is determined by the amount of land and materials it requires to produce the resources it consumes and absorb the waste it generates. Due to concepts like global trading this is not necessarily just a countryâ€™s own land and sea area. In these visualisations, the ecological footprint is expressed as resource use/footprint compared to the resource capacity/biocapacity of the earth, resulting in a reserve or deficit of resources. The footprint per country is expressed as the amount of earths required if the whole world produced and consumed like that country. The relation between footprint and other variables can be inspected in the scatter plot. Agricultural land (as % of total land) and livestock production (index with values for the years 2004-2006 as a base value of 100) were included because they are directly related to resource use and production. Population density (people per sq. km of land area) and globalisation (index) were included as social factors that may be associated with the ecological footprint of a country.
